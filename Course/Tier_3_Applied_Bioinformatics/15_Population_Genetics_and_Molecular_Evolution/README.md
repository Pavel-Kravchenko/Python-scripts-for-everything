# Module 15: Population Genetics and Molecular Evolution

This module covers the mathematical and computational foundations of how allele frequencies change over time under drift, selection, and mutation.
Students simulate Wright-Fisher drift across population sizes, compute selection coefficients, and contrast neutral theory with adaptive evolution.
The molecular clock, Jukes-Cantor distance correction, and divergence-time estimation are derived from first principles with worked examples.
dN/dS (Ka/Ks) ratios are computed step by step using the Nei-Gojobori method and applied to a sliding window along a viral gene.
Neutrality tests (Tajima's D, McDonald-Kreitman) and population structure metrics (Fst, linkage disequilibrium) close the module with four applied exercises.
