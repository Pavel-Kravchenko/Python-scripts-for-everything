# Module 13: Biochemistry and Enzyme Kinetics

This module covers the quantitative analysis of enzyme activity using classical kinetic models including Michaelis-Menten, inhibition kinetics, and allosteric regulation.
Students implement curve-fitting pipelines to extract kinetic parameters (Km, Vmax, Ki, Hill coefficient) from synthetic and experimental data.
Linearization methods (Lineweaver-Burk, Eadie-Hofstee, Hanes-Woolf) are derived and compared to understand their statistical limitations.
Allosteric enzymes are explored through the Hill equation, with hemoglobin O2 binding as a worked example.
The module closes with metabolic pathway stoichiometry, flux balance analysis concepts, and four applied exercises.
